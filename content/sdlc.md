Title: System Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

Each phase below corresponds to a stage in the timeline above, shown with the gate that must be cleared to advance to the next phase.

* ### **Discovery**

  *Timeline:* Research and planning to understand your users & define the Core Problems that need to be solved

* Your Discovery phase includes research and planning to understand your users and define the Core Problems that need to be solved. Most of the questions in your Launchpad are directly related to what you should be able to learn in Discovery. This phase is formally completed upon the submission and approval of your MITDP Launchpad.

* **Gate: Approval of Launchpad**
  Review your Launchpad and approve your project as an MITDP. At this point, the project proceeds to an optional Alpha phase, or directly to Prep and 90-Day Build.

* ### **Alpha** *(optional)*

  *Timeline:* Test and evaluate different technology options to see what might be best
  *Milestone:* MITDP approved for Alpha phase

* This is an optional phase to test and evaluate different technology options to see what might be best. The Alpha phase is recommended when you're not confident which technology might be best. As an MITDP, you'll have access to budget and oversight support to test possible options with real-world use cases and determine what solutions might be the best fit.

* **Gate: Approval of updated Launchpad**
  If your project conducted an Alpha phase, this gate reviews your updated Launchpad so the project can proceed to Prep and 90-Day Build.

* ### **Prep**

  *Timeline:* Prepare contracts, staffing, & operational needs to be ready for your 90-Day Build
  *Milestone:* MITDP approved for Prep phase

* In the Prep phase, you'll have time to prepare contracts, staffing, and operational needs to be ready to begin delivering software. The duration of this phase is based on the requirements you listed in your Definition of Ready, which is part of the Implementation Strategy worksheet. When all of the items in your Definition of Ready are addressed, the clock will start for your 90-Day Build.

* **Gate: Completion of Prep — 90-Day clock starts**
  Confirm that all items in your Definition of Ready are addressed, and begin the clock for your 90-Day Build.

* ### **90-Day Build**

  *Timeline:* Build a small slice of functionality in a demonstration environment
  *Milestone:* Countdown starts for 90-Day Build

* In this phase, your team will build a small slice of functionality to demonstrate its ability to produce working software within 90 days, and develop a prioritized backlog of features to address during the Delivery phase. This requirement helps ensure that all MITDP teams have the skills and resources to succeed, provides early indicators where there are challenges to address, and encourages teams to start small and work iteratively. It should NOT include the full scope of your MITDP. You'll define the scope of your 90-Day Build in the Implementation Strategy worksheet, which also includes space to define how you will host the working software so that both the user experience and the quality of the underlying code can be evaluated. (For example, you could share a password for a development environment to view the working software and a link to a code repository such as GitHub to see the underlying code.)

* **Gate: Approval of 90-Day Build**
  Confirm that the team is able to build working software within its first 90 days. If the team was not able to, specific adjustments to the team or its working processes may be recommended, with an opportunity for another 90-Day Build once those are met. The team's backlog of features for the Delivery phase is also reviewed here.

* ### **Delivery**

  *Timeline:* Build out functionality to meet success metrics for all Core Problems
  *Milestone:* Gate Review of 90-Day Build

* This is the core phase of your MITDP. It continues until you've completed functionality to meet success metrics for all of your project's Core Problems.

* **Gate: Success metrics met for all Core Problems**
  Confirm that the project has launched and tested functionality to meet success metrics for all of its Core Problems. Following this confirmation, the project has one more year as an MITDP to transition to agency funding and oversight.

* ### **Transition Year**

  *Timeline:* Adjust team, contracts, & budget to support ongoing continuous improvement after MITDP closeout
  *Milestone:* Success metrics met for all Core Problems

* Space to adjust team staffing, contracts, and budgets to support continuous improvement following MITDP closeout.

* **Gate: MITDP Closeout**
  Close out the project as an MITDP.

* ### **Post-MITDP Continuous Improvement**

  *Timeline:* Continue to improve the product for as long as it has users
  *Milestone:* MITDP closeout

* Continued iteration and improvement based on user needs. A dedicated team should continue to be assigned to oversee and improve the product for as long as it continues to have users.
